About

The primary goal of newscycle.report is to provide a non-distracting user interface for consuming and scanning news headlines at scale. This means a UI with a high information density (signal-to-noise ratio), allowing users to scan headlines from multiple news sources at once and form a broad context of related story items. For example, this service was being developed when President Biden dropped out of the 2024 presidential race (the week of July 21st). The ability to view breaking news headlines from sources like `Zero Hedge`, `The Economist`, `Financial Times`, `The Atlantic`, `Breitbart`, and others on the same screen allows users to gain a wider context of what is happening.

Furthermore, this web application offers several features in addition to its high information density:

  • Easily search through all subscribed news sources by searching the text on the current page, using built-in browser functionality for simplicity.
  • Save individual news articles into personalized curated lists that users can either share publicly or keep private.
  • Customize the news feeds that appear on an authenticated user's front page.
  • Collapse or expand individual news sources as needed to display only a subset of subscribed sources.
  • Provide a non-bloated, minimal front-end that uses vanilla JavaScript with no large dependencies to download.
  • Adopt a word-of-mouth membership model, focusing on non-profit aims and utilitarianism rather than rapid user growth.

Any new features added to this web application will be documented here with updates noted by their date of release. The remainder of this document further details the major features summarized above.
